— All-road: these motorcycles have longer suspension travel than "standard" road bikes, yet they typically feature 17-inch front tires to prioritize on-road handling over off-road performance.

Typical motorcycles in this category are the Yamaha Tracer 7/9, Kawasaki Versys 650/1100, and Ducati Multistrada V4.

— Off-road: Motorcycles in this category often feature longer suspension travel than all-road motorcycles, as well as 19–21-inch front tires. They are also frequently equipped with handguards, skid plates, and crash bars.

The most common motorcycles in this category are the BMW R 1300 GS, Yamaha Ténéré 700, and Honda XL750 Transalp.

— Dual Sport: Motorbikes in this category move a step closer to pure enduro bikes compared to off-road models. Long suspension travel, a 21-inch front tire, and low weight are common characteristics of bikes in this category.

Typical motorcycles in this category are the KTM 690 Enduro R, Suzuki DR-Z4S, and Honda CRF 300 L.
